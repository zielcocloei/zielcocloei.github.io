Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and the celestial objects bound to it by gravity, including eight planets. Each planet has unique characteristics that make it special.

Sun

The Sun is the center of the Solar System and a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma. It provides light and energy, sustaining life on Earth.

Mercury

The closest planet to the Sun, Mercury is a small, rocky planet with extreme temperature variations and no atmosphere to retain heat.

Venus

Known as Earth's twin, Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere and the hottest surface temperature among all planets due to a runaway greenhouse effect.

Earth

The only planet known to support life, Earth has liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

Often called the Red Planet, Mars has a cold, desert-like environment with the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.

Jupiter

The largest planet in the Solar System, Jupiter is a gas giant with a Great Red Spot, a massive storm system visible for centuries.

Saturn

Famous for its stunning ring system, Saturn is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.

Uranus

A gas giant with a bluish color due to methane in its atmosphere, Uranus rotates on its side, making it unique among the planets.

Neptune

The farthest planet from the Sun, Neptune is a windy, cold planet known for its deep blue color and intense storms.
